Biography of Lewis Hamilton


Sir Lewis Hamilton, a British racing driver born on January 7, 1985, is widely regarded as one of the greatest Formula 1 drivers in history.

From a young age, his immense talent was evident; he began karting at eight and, at just ten, won the British Cadet Karting Championship, catching the attention of McLaren team boss Ron Dennis.

Hamilton made his F1 debut with McLaren in 2007, and in his second season, he became the youngest world champion at the time.
He later moved to Mercedes in 2013, a decision that proved to be a masterstroke.With Mercedes, Hamilton secured six more world championships, bringing his total to a joint-record seven, tied with Michael Schumacher.

Beyond his unprecedented success on the track, including records for most wins, pole positions, and podiums, Hamilton has become a global icon for his activism.

He uses his platform (Mission 44) to advocate for diversity and inclusion in motorsport, racial justice, and environmental causes.

He was knighted in 2021 for his contributions to the sport.
